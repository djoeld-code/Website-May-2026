Privacy Policy

Rediscovered Purpose Counselling Services · Dave Dyck, MC, CCC · Last updated: April 2026
Your privacy is not just a legal obligation — it is a cornerstone of the trust we build together. This policy explains how Rediscovered Purpose Counselling Services collects, uses, and protects your personal information in accordance with the BC Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) and the Canadian Counselling and Psychotherapy Association (CCPA) Code of Ethics.

1 Who We Are

Rediscovered Purpose Counselling Services is operated by Dave Dyck, MC, CCC, a Canadian Certified Counsellor registered with the Canadian Counselling and Psychotherapy Association (CCPA). Services are provided in-person in Nanaimo and across Vancouver Island (beginning mid-June 2026), and virtually to clients throughout British Columbia.

Contact: davedyck@rediscoveredpurposecounselling.ca · 1 (250) 274-3126

2 Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact information — your name, email address, and phone number, provided when you submit a booking request or contact form.
  • Session-related information — details you share about your reasons for seeking counselling, your goals, and your personal history, shared voluntarily during sessions.
  • Insurance and billing information — extended health benefit plan details if you choose direct billing, or autism funding information if applicable.
  • Technical information — basic website usage data (e.g., page visits) may be collected through standard web hosting tools. No personally identifiable tracking is used.

3 How We Use Your Information

Your personal information is used only for the purposes for which it was collected, including:

  • Responding to your session inquiry or booking request
  • Scheduling and confirming appointments
  • Providing counselling services tailored to your needs
  • Processing direct billing to insurance providers or autism funding bodies
  • Communicating important updates about your sessions

We will not use your information for marketing purposes, sell it to third parties, or share it without your explicit consent, except as required by law.

4 Confidentiality & Limits of Confidentiality

Everything discussed within counselling sessions is strictly confidential. As a CCPA-registered counsellor, Dave Dyck adheres to the CCPA Code of Ethics regarding client confidentiality.

Limits of confidentiality: There are specific situations where confidentiality may be legally required to be broken, including: (1) where there is imminent risk of serious harm to yourself or another identifiable person; (2) where abuse or neglect of a child or vulnerable adult is suspected; or (3) where a court order compels disclosure. These limits will be clearly explained at the beginning of your first session.

5 Storage & Protection of Your Information

Your personal information is stored securely and protected against unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. We take reasonable physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information.

  • Client session notes are stored in a secure, password-protected system accessible only to Dave Dyck.
  • Email communications are conducted through a private professional email account.
  • Paper records, if any, are stored in a locked, secure location.

Client records are retained for a minimum of 7 years following the end of the therapeutic relationship, as required under BC professional standards. Records related to minors are retained until the individual reaches age 25, or for 7 years after the last session, whichever is longer.

6 Virtual Sessions & Technology

Virtual counselling sessions are conducted using secure, encrypted video platforms. Clients are responsible for ensuring they are in a private location during virtual sessions to protect their own confidentiality.

We recommend that clients do not participate in sessions from shared or public spaces. Session recordings are never made without your explicit written consent.

7 Insurance & Third-Party Billing

If you choose to use direct billing to an extended health insurance provider, ICBC (for motor vehicle accident claims), or to access autism funding, limited personal and session information will be shared with the relevant third party as required to process the claim. This may include your name, date of birth, claim number, session dates, and service codes. You will be informed of exactly what information is shared before any submission is made.

8 Your Rights Under BC PIPA

Under the BC Personal Information Protection Act, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction — request that inaccurate information be corrected
  • Withdrawal of consent — withdraw consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of your information (subject to legal and ethical obligations)
  • Complaint — file a complaint with the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner for BC if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

To exercise any of these rights, please contact Dave Dyck directly using the contact information below.

9 Website & Cookies

This website does not use tracking cookies or third-party advertising. The contact and booking form on this site collects only the information you voluntarily provide. This information is transmitted securely and used solely to respond to your inquiry.

This site may be hosted on a third-party platform (such as Netlify). Basic, anonymized site traffic data may be collected by the hosting provider in accordance with their own privacy policies.

10 Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in practice, technology, or legal requirements. The current version will always be available on this website. Continued use of our services following any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Questions About Your Privacy?

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how your personal information is handled, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'm committed to being transparent and responsive.
