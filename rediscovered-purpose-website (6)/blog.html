5 Signs You Might Benefit from Counselling

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 4 min read 📍 Nanaimo, BC

Many people wait until they're in crisis before reaching out for support. But counselling isn't just for rock-bottom moments — it's for anyone who wants to live more fully, feel better, or work through something that's been weighing on them. Here are five signs that talking to a counsellor might help you.

1. You Feel Stuck and Don't Know Why

You're going through the motions but something feels off. Maybe you can't quite name what's wrong — you just know that life feels heavier than it should. This kind of low-grade unhappiness is one of the most common reasons people seek counselling, and it's one of the most responsive to treatment.

2. Anxiety is Interfering with Your Daily Life

Worry is a normal part of life. But when anxiety starts affecting your sleep, your relationships, your work, or your ability to enjoy things — that's a signal worth paying attention to. Anxiety is highly treatable, and many people see significant improvement within just a few sessions using evidence-based approaches.

3. You're Struggling After a Difficult Event

Whether it's a car accident, a loss, a relationship breakdown, or a major life change — difficult events can leave marks that don't heal on their own. If you find yourself replaying events, avoiding situations, or feeling unlike yourself after something hard happened, counselling can help you process and move forward.

4. Your Relationships Feel Strained

Communication breakdowns, conflict, disconnection — relationship struggles are painful and exhausting. Counselling gives you tools to communicate more effectively, understand your patterns, and rebuild connection — whether that's in couples counselling or individual work focused on your relationships.

5. You've Been Told "Just Push Through It"

This one is especially common for men. The pressure to tough it out, stay strong, and not ask for help is real — and it keeps a lot of people suffering longer than they need to. Asking for support isn't weakness. It's one of the most effective things you can do for your mental health, your relationships, and your quality of life.

How ICBC Counselling Works After a Car Accident in BC

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 📍 Vancouver Island, BC

Being involved in a motor vehicle accident (MVA) is traumatic — even when there are no serious physical injuries. The emotional and psychological aftermath of a car accident is real, significant, and often overlooked. As a registered ICBC counselling provider on Vancouver Island, I want to help you understand how to access mental health support after an MVA in BC.

What Happens to Your Mental Health After an MVA?

After a car accident, it's common to experience a range of emotional and psychological responses including:

  • Anxiety and hypervigilance — especially while driving or riding in vehicles
  • Flashbacks and intrusive thoughts — replaying the accident involuntarily
  • Sleep disturbances — difficulty falling or staying asleep
  • Irritability and mood changes — feeling on edge or easily frustrated
  • Emotional regulation difficulties — especially common after concussion
  • Avoidance behaviours — avoiding driving, certain roads, or conversations about the accident

What is Concussion Counselling?

Concussion after an MVA affects far more than the brain physically — it impacts mood, emotional regulation, anxiety, and cognitive function. Many people don't realize that counselling is a recognized and effective part of concussion recovery. I provide specialized emotional regulation techniques designed specifically for post-concussion support, helping clients manage the emotional symptoms that often accompany brain injury recovery.

How Does ICBC Cover Counselling?

If you've been in a motor vehicle accident in BC and have an active ICBC claim, you may be entitled to counselling coverage. As a registered ICBC service provider, I can direct bill ICBC directly — meaning in many cases there is no upfront cost to you. Here's how it generally works:

  • Report your accident to ICBC and open a claim if you haven't already
  • Get a referral from your doctor or healthcare provider recommending counselling
  • Contact me — I'll confirm your coverage and handle the billing directly with ICBC
  • Begin sessions — focused on your recovery and emotional wellbeing

Do I Need to Have Physical Injuries to Access ICBC Counselling?

No. Psychological injuries from a motor vehicle accident are recognized by ICBC and can be covered under your claim even without significant physical injuries. The emotional impact of an accident is valid and deserving of support regardless of what happened to your body.

If you've been in a car accident on Vancouver Island or anywhere in BC and are struggling emotionally, I'm here to help. Contact me to discuss your ICBC claim and how we can get you the support you deserve — with direct billing so cost isn't a barrier.

Why Men Don't Ask for Help — And Why That Needs to Change

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 4 min read 📍 Nanaimo, BC

Men in Canada are significantly less likely to seek mental health support than women — even when they're struggling just as much. As a male counsellor in Nanaimo, I provide a space welcoming to everyone, with a particular understanding of the barriers men often face. This article is for anyone who has ever thought "I should be able to handle this on my own."

Why Men Avoid Counselling

The reasons men avoid seeking help are deeply rooted and worth understanding — not judging:

  • The "toughen up" message — most men grew up being told that asking for help is a sign of weakness, not strength
  • Not knowing what to expect — counselling can feel unfamiliar or uncomfortable when you've never tried it
  • Fear of judgment — worry about what a therapist, or others, might think
  • Not recognizing the symptoms — men often express depression and anxiety differently, through anger, withdrawal, or overworking rather than sadness
  • Practical barriers — time, cost, and not knowing where to start

What Men's Counselling Actually Looks Like

Counselling with me isn't lying on a couch talking about your feelings for an hour. It's direct, practical, and goal-oriented. We identify what's not working, figure out why, and build concrete strategies to change it. Whether you're dealing with anxiety, relationship stress, work pressure, anger, or a life transition — we approach it the same way you'd approach any other problem worth solving.

The Cost of Not Getting Help

Men who don't address mental health struggles don't just suffer themselves — the people around them feel it too. Relationships strain. Work suffers. Physical health declines. The stoic approach has a real cost, and it compounds over time. Getting support isn't giving up — it's one of the most effective investments you can make in every area of your life.

Why a Male Counsellor Matters

Many men find it easier to open up with another man who gets it — someone who understands the pressures, the expectations, and the internal conflict between wanting help and feeling like you shouldn't need it. As a male counsellor in Nanaimo, I bring that understanding to every session. You don't have to explain why it's hard. I already know.
